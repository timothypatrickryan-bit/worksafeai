# PROJECT WARP SPEED
## Capability Assessment & Hiring Plan
### Pro-Tel Infrastructure Growth Acceleration

**Prepared:** March 28, 2026  
**For:** Tim Ryan (CEO, Pro-Tel)  
**Deadline:** April 2, 2026  
**Document Version:** 1.0 - Final  

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Pro-Tel is positioned at an **inflection point**. The Northeast data center infrastructure market is experiencing unprecedented growth ($5.9B-7.7B TAM, 22-25% CAGR through 2028), driven by AI infrastructure buildout, hyperscaler expansion, and edge computing deployment. 

**Current State:** Pro-Tel is a **small regional player** with strong technical foundations but significant gaps in sales leadership, market positioning, delivery scale, and organizational structure needed for market leadership.

**Opportunity:** With disciplined execution on hiring and organizational restructuring, Pro-Tel can grow from a **sub-$50M regional operator** (2025) to an **$80-120M market leader** (2028) in Northeast data center infrastructure.

**Success Requires:** 
1. **Aggressive hiring** (18-24 people over 12 months, not 6)
2. **Specialized team structure** with clear accountability
3. **Sales & business development** leadership (critical gap today)
4. **Delivery & operations scaling** (3-5x current capacity)
5. **Strategic partnerships** with Tier-1 suppliers (Corning, Panduit)

   - ❌ No dedicated sales leadership (no VP/Director of Sales)
   - ❌ Limited account management structure

   - ❌ No thought leadership/content strategy
   - ❌ Limited brand awareness in target segments
   - ❌ Weak marketing/PR function
   - **Impact:** Difficult to compete with Tier-1 players; low win rates

### 3.3 CRITICAL ROLE DEEP DIVES

#### **ROLE #1: VP OF SALES** (CRITICAL #1 - Hire April 2026)

**Why Critical:** Pro-Tel lacks sales leadership. No one is accountable for revenue, pipeline, customer relationships, win rates. This is the #1 blocker to growth.

**Profile:**
- **Experience:** 15+ years in enterprise infrastructure/technology sales
- **Background:** Ideal if from telecom, datacenter, or IT services background
- **Size:** Has sold deals $5M-50M+ range
- **Track Record:** Grew sales from <$50M to $100M+ revenue; managed teams of 5-10 AEs
- **Personality:** Coachable, data-driven, customer-obsessed, not ego-driven

**Key Responsibilities:**
1. Define sales strategy & positioning (what are we selling, to whom, for how much)
2. Recruit & manage sales team (AEs, SDRs, sales ops)
3. Develop sales playbook (discovery, qualification, RFP response, close)
4. Manage major accounts (hyperscaler relationships, strategic deals)
5. Build pipeline forecasting & metrics
6. Define customer success/retention strategy
7. Compensation plan design & incentives
8. Sales training & enablement

**Success Metrics (6-month):**
- ✅ Sales team of 3-4 AEs hired & onboarded
- ✅ Sales playbook documented (discovery, qualification, RFP)
- ✅ $2-3M pipeline built (qualified opportunities)
- ✅ Sales org metrics defined (ACV, sales cycle, win rate, CAC)
- ✅ 1-2 major contracts signed (reference customers)
- ✅ Commission/compensation plan in place
- ✅ Sales forecast model working

**Red Flags to Avoid:**
- ❌ Has only sold software/SaaS (infrastructure deals different; longer sales cycles, different buyers)
- ❌ Lacks experience managing enterprise sales cycles (12-24 months)
- ❌ Weak at developing reps (hires but doesn't coach)
- ❌ Overly focused on deals vs. team/process
- ❌ History of high turnover (toxic leadership)

**Salary Band:** $150-180K base + equity (if founder-backed) or 10-15% upside target-based bonus

**Interview Questions:**
1. "Tell me about the largest deal you've closed. What made it win?"
2. "Describe your sales process. How do you qualify, develop, and close enterprise deals?"
3. "What does a healthy sales organization look like? What KPIs matter most?"
4. "How do you build and coach a high-performing sales team?"
5. "What's your experience with infrastructure/datacenter sales? Why does it matter?"

---

#### **ROLE #2: ACCOUNT EXECUTIVE** (CRITICAL #2-3 - Hire April-May 2026, 2 people)

**Why Critical:** Revenue can't grow without salespeople who can close deals. Pro-Tel needs 2-3 AEs immediately to pursue hyperscaler, regional DC, and colocation contracts.

**Profile (AE #1 - Enterprise/Hyperscaler Focus):**
- **Experience:** 7-10 years in enterprise infrastructure/tech sales
- **Background:** Ideally from hyperscaler (AWS, Google, Meta) or large systems integrator (Accenture, IBM, CapGemini)
- **Specialty:** Can navigate complex RFPs, manage multi-stakeholder deals, close $5-20M+ contracts
- **Territory:** Focus on hyperscalers (Amazon, Microsoft, Google facilities in PA/NY)
- **Personality:** Relationship-builder, process-driven, coachable, persistence

**Profile (AE #2 - Regional/Colocation Focus):**
- **Experience:** 5-7 years in regional infrastructure or colocation sales
- **Background:** Could be from Digital Realty, Equinix, Iron Mountain, or regional integrator
- **Specialty:** Mid-market facilities (50-200MW), colocation providers, regional DCs
- **Territory:** Focus on regional growth; expand existing customer relationships
- **Personality:** Territory builder, referral-focused, customer-obsessed

**Key Responsibilities:**
1. Identify & qualify prospective customers
2. Navigate complex RFP processes (hyperscaler: 12-24 month sales cycle)
3. Present technical solutions; coordinate with engineers/project managers
4. Negotiate contracts, pricing, terms
5. Close deals; hand off to delivery/project management
6. Maintain customer relationships post-sale (upsell, retention)
7. Build pipeline; forecast quarterly revenue
8. Participate in industry events, networking

**Success Metrics (6-month):**
- ✅ $500K-1M in signed contracts (both AEs combined)
- ✅ $1-2M in pipeline (qualified, advancing toward close)
- ✅ 3-5 active deals in sales cycle
- ✅ Win rate 20-25% (normal for infrastructure)
- ✅ 2-3 reference customers for hyperscaler/regional DC market
- ✅ Strong relationship with 5-10 prospect decision-makers
- ✅ Forecasted revenue per AE: $5-10M annually

**Sourcing Strategy:**
1. **Network with VP Sales hire** (candidate likely has referrals)
2. **Target current infrastructure salespeople** from larger competitors (CommScope, Panduit resellers, integrators)
3. **Hyperscaler-ex talent** (AWS, Google infrastructure recruiting; know the customer, buying process)
4. **Referral bonus** ($5-10K finder's fee if VP brings referrals)
5. **Recruiter engagement** (specialized recruiting firms for infrastructure/telecom sales)

**Salary Band:** $90-130K base + $30-50K OTE (on-target earnings) commission = $120-180K total at quota

## SECTION 9: RISK MITIGATION & CONTINGENCY PLANNING

### Scenario 1: VP Sales Recruitment Delays

**Risk:** Can't find the right sales leader (happens often in growth companies)

**Contingency:**
1. Interim: Hire strong AEs first (they can operate without a manager initially)
2. Bring in sales consultant (fractional VP for 90 days)
3. Promote sales operations manager to interim VP (if capable)
4. Extend timeline: Start with 2 AEs, add third later when VP ready
5. Adjust targets: $15-20M revenue vs. $25M if VP delayed 2-3 months

### Scenario 2: Project Pipeline Slower Than Expected

**Risk:** Sales take longer than expected; revenue misses

**Contingency:**
1. Increase SDR effort (if hired) or hiring SDR immediately
2. Accelerate colocation/regional DC sales (shorter cycles than hyperscale)
3. Extend team ramp: Delay Field Technician hiring if project pipeline weak
4. Cost control: Reduce discretionary spending; tighten margin expectations
5. Board communication: Adjust expectations transparently; pivot strategy if needed

### Scenario 3: Delivery Capacity Misses

**Risk:** Can't scale operations fast enough to support sales

**Contingency:**
1. Subcontract work to regional partners (maintain margin via partner arrangement)
2. Hire more technicians immediately (tap union/temp agencies for short-term)
3. Parallel project management (assign more PM capacity if project work backs up)
4. Partner with Panduit or other vendors on design-build (split revenue but get expertise)

### Scenario 4: Key Person Leaves (VP Sales, VP Ops)

**Risk:** Critical hire leaves after 6 months (bad fit, better opportunity, etc.)

**Contingency:**
1. Cross-training: Always have #2 person trained on critical functions
2. Documentation: Processes, relationships, sales playbook in writing (not just in person's head)
3. Retention incentives: Signing bonuses with clawback; equity vesting
4. Immediate replacement plan: Start recruiting before person indicates they're leaving
5. Consulting arrangement: Keep departing leader as advisor (6-month transition)
