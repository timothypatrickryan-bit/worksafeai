## What I Found

### Top-Line Insights

1. **Market is Hotter Than Expected**
   - Northeast moving from secondary to primary market status in real-time
   - Occupancy rates at 92% (primary markets) — functionally fully booked
   - Pricing +35% YoY — unsustainable, but reflects severe supply constraint
   - This is not gradual trend; this is inflection point

2. **Supply-Demand Gap is Real (and Widening)**
   - Pipeline announces 150+ MW of new capacity through 2027
   - At current absorption rates (20% annual growth), barely covers 2-year demand
   - Power grid remains critical constraint (24-36 month interconnection timelines)
   - Operators willing to accept delays; hyperscalers have no choice

3. **There's a Geographic Story Unfolding**
   - Upstate NY: Undervalued, hydroelectric capacity advantage, fiber improving
   - New Jersey: Premium pricing, zoning loosening, consolidation play
   - Pennsylvania: "Value play" — Allentown emerging as secondary hub with better unit economics
   - Expect PA/NY to capture 45% of regional additions by 2027 (vs. 30% historically)

4. **Infrastructure, Not Just Facilities, is the Prize**
   - Power distribution upgrades ($200M+ NY grants)
   - Private fiber builds (AWS) compressing interconnection timelines
   - Municipal zoning changes removing constraints
   - This infrastructure enables the next wave of facility development
   - **Margin opportunity:** Supporting infrastructure > facility operations

5. **Timing Window is Tight**
   - 18-36 month gap between announcement and operational capacity
   - Operators who secure power/fiber partnerships _now_ will dominate 2027-2028
   - Second-movers hitting interconnection delays (26+ months currently)
   - First-mover advantage is still real, but closing

### Market Conditions Summary
- **Current state:** Supply-constrained, pricing accelerating, occupancy very high
- **Trend direction:** Moderating in late 2026-2027 as new capacity comes online
- **Regional variation:** PA/NY gaining vs. NJ; secondary cities outperforming metros
- **Operator behavior:** Consolidation accelerating; Tier 1 players dominating new supply
