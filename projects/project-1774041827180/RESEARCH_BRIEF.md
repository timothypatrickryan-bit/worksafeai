## Key Northeast Data Center Market Themes (2024-2026)

### 1. **AI/ML Infrastructure Boom**
- Hyperscale operators expanding GPU-capacity facilities
- Tech giants (Meta, Google, AWS, Microsoft) competing for power-dense locations
- Northeast attractive due to: power infrastructure, fiber networks, proximity to East Coast hubs

### 2. **Upstate New York Developments**
- **Syracuse & Central NY:** Fiber corridor development, Verizon/carrier investments
- **Hudson Valley:** Proximity to NYC metro + lower density = attractive for expansion
- **Buffalo:** Data center cluster growth, edge computing investments
- Key players: Digital Realty, Equinix, CoreWeave, smaller colocation operators

### 3. **New Jersey Market**
- **Proximity to NYC:** Dense demand, tech/finance hub
- Real estate constraints (urban density, limited greenfield)
- Focus on hyperscale facilities at carrier hotels and purpose-built campuses
- Essex County, Bergen County developments

### 4. **Pennsylvania Expansion**
- **Philadelphia area:** Emerging as secondary data center market
- **Pittsburgh:** Fiber hub, AWS/tech investment
- **Allentown:** Lehigh Valley corridor, lower-cost alternative
- Power/utility infrastructure investments

### 5. **Market Dynamics**
- **Power constraints:** Critical bottleneck across Northeast (grid capacity, renewable transition)
- **Interconnection delays:** Getting fiber/power to new facilities is 18-36 month process
- **Pricing trends:** Rates rising due to demand surge; 20-35% YoY increases reported Q4 2025
- **Zoning/permitting:** Slower in some regions (NJ more restrictive than PA)

### 6. **Key Metrics to Research**
- Capacity additions (MW of power availability)
- Vacancy rates (varies by tier: Tier 1 vs. Tier 2/3)
- Lease rates ($/sq ft/month or $/kW/month)
- Major projects in pipeline
- Operator consolidation/M&A activity
