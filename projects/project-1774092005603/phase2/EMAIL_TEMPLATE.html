Hyperscaler Capex Hits $700B—Capacity Still Lags Demand

📊 Data Center Knowledge 📅 Mar 17, 2026

Total capital expenditure by six major US hyperscalers—Microsoft, AWS, Meta, Google, Oracle, and CoreWeave—is expected to reach approximately $700 billion in 2026, nearly six times 2022 levels, according to Moody's Ratings. Even at this unprecedented level, the industry remains capacity-constrained: demand for compute capacity to train new AI models and support growing inference continues to exceed supply through 2027.

Why it matters: Signals sustained, multi-year AI infrastructure commitment. Capacity shortfall persists despite $700B capex—market undersupplied. Companies increasingly relying on debt financing as free cash flow erodes.

Key numbers:
  • Capex: ~$120B (2022) → ~$700B (2026)
  • Supply gap: Electricity + construction timelines constrain supply through 2027
  • Major spenders: Microsoft, AWS, Meta, Google each $40B+

LightSpeed + Infraeo Partner on NPO Fiber for AI Interconnects

🌐 Data Center Knowledge 📅 Mar 20, 2026

Singapore-based LightSpeed Photonics and Texas-based Infraeo announced a memorandum of understanding to develop next-generation interconnect technologies (near-packaged optics) for hyperscale AI data centers. NPO provides a middle path between copper limits and co-packaged optics complexity, offering low-latency, high-bandwidth connections for disaggregated AI infrastructure.

Why it matters: AI bottleneck shifting from compute to data movement. NPO filling critical gap. Supply chain diversification to India signals confidence in distributed manufacturing. US pilot deployments suggest 2026-2027 commercialization timeline.

Partnership focus:
  • NPO, PCIe-over-fiber, low-latency interconnects
  • US customer pilots + India-based supply chain
  • Go-to-market expansion in 2026
