### **5.2 Cross-Validation Process**

**Rule:** Do not cite a fact in DCWU without:
1. Verification from at least 2 independent sources, OR
2. Official source (permit database, press release, earnings call), OR
3. Direct Pro-Tel customer/partner confirmation

**Conflict Resolution:**
- If sources disagree on timing/capacity: cite both, flag uncertainty
- If announcement lacks detail: mark as "pending clarity," note expected update timing
- If news is speculative: label "reported" not "confirmed"; cite source expectation

### **5.3 Freshness Standards**

- **Major announcements:** <24 hours old (if verified from 2+ sources)
- **Market analysis:** <1 week old
- **Regulatory changes:** <2 weeks old (must cite source date)
- **Competitive intelligence:** <1 month old
- **Trend analysis:** <3 months old (note as "developing")
- **Do not include:** Unverified rumors, speculative projections without sourcing, opinion without attribution

## 6. CITATION & SOURCE VALIDATION REQUIREMENTS

### **6.1 Citation Format**

Every fact in DCWU must include:
1. **Source name** (DataCenterKnowledge, JLL Report, etc.)
2. **Publication date** (MM/DD/26 format)
3. **Headline or summary** (brief)
4. **URL** (if digital; use shortlinks if necessary)

**Example:**
> "Equinix announced expansion of its NY4 facility by 5MW (Google Cloud Blog, 03/15/26: 'Equinix Expands Northeast Capacity for AI Workloads')"

### **6.2 Fact-Checking Checklist**

Before including any fact:
- [ ] Source is in the Qualified Sources list (Tiers 1-5)?
- [ ] Fact is <30 days old (or is evergreen analysis)?
- [ ] Fact is verified by 2+ independent sources OR official source?
- [ ] If speculative, labeled as "projected," "announced," "reported" (not "confirmed")?
- [ ] Citation includes date, source, URL?
- [ ] Bias acknowledged (e.g., "Equinix announced [company-favorable statement]")?

### **6.3 Red Flags (Do Not Include Without Escalation)**

- "Sources say" / "unnamed sources" (unless from Tier 1 publication)
- Rumors from social media without verification
- Projections >1 year out without source attribution
- Contradictory data from sources (flag in draft for editor review)
- Information older than 3 months (unless evergreen analysis)
- Unattributed "will expand" or "plans to" statements

## 7. QUALITY STANDARDS & EDITORIAL GATES

### **7.1 Readability & Professional Tone**

- **Audience:** Tim Ryan + Pro-Tel stakeholders (technical, business-savvy, outcome-focused)
- **Length:** 800-1,200 words per weekly email
- **Style:** Direct, analytical, actionable (not sensationalist)
- **Terminology:** Industry-standard (avoid jargon without definition)
- **Structure:** Executive summary → Key developments → Trends → Pro-Tel relevance

### **7.2 Pre-Publication Validation (Steven's Review)**

Steven (the writer) should:
1. Cross-check all citations against source URLs
2. Flag any dated/stale information
3. Verify geographic accuracy (confirm facility location is in upstate NY, NJ, or PA)
4. Ensure Pro-Tel relevance (remove generic industry noise)
5. Test all links before sending
6. Confirm tone matches professional standard (no hype, hyperbole, or unsubstantiated claims)

### **7.3 Monthly Accuracy Audit**

Once per month:
1. Review past 4 weeks of DCWU emails
2. Validate that announced timelines are tracking (or update if changed)
3. Check if any sources have issued corrections/updates
4. Document lessons learned (e.g., "JLL reports always lag by 2 weeks; adjust planning")
5. Update template/process if needed

## 11. NOTES FOR STEVEN (THE WRITER)

**Key Reminders:**
1. **Pro-Tel focus:** Every section should connect to Pro-Tel's expansion goals. If a data point doesn't impact Pro-Tel, consider cutting it.
2. **Geographic discipline:** Only upstate NY, NJ, PA. National trends are background context, not the headline.
3. **Actionable insight:** Aim for 1-2 clear takeaways per email (e.g., "Power grid constraint creating opportunity in central PA").

5. **Verify links:** Before sending, click every URL. Broken links kill credibility.
6. **Tone:** Professional, not sensational. "Equinix expands" (not "Equinix surges"). This is analysis, not marketing hype.
7. **Be transparent about bias:** If citing a company announcement, note that it's company-favorable (e.g., "Equinix reported a 20% increase in Northeast utilization").
