## Common Citation Formats

**News Announcement:**
> Equinix announced a 5MW expansion of its NY4 facility to support AI workloads (Equinix Newsroom, 03/15/26)

**Market Analysis:**
> Data center leasing velocity in Northern New Jersey increased 12% YoY (JLL Q1 2026 Northeast DC Market Report, 03/10/26)

**Company Guidance:**
> Google plans to expand cloud infrastructure in upstate NY through 2027 (Google Cloud Blog, 03/20/26: "Northeast Infrastructure Investment")

**Regulatory/Permit:**
> New Jersey issued permits for a 3MW colocation facility in Secaucus (NJ.gov Permit Database, filed 03/18/26)

## Red Flags (Don't Include Without Escalation to Tim)

❌ "Sources say" (unless from DCK editorial)
❌ LinkedIn rumors (verify with press release first)
❌ Projections >1 year out (unless from earnings call)
❌ Info >3 months old (unless evergreen analysis)
❌ Contradictory data (flag for Tim to resolve)
❌ Unconfirmed facility names or locations
❌ Operator statements without press release (cite source clearly)

---

## Pro-Tel Positioning Language

**When to mention Pro-Tel relevance:**
- "This expansion signals demand Pro-Tel can address in [region]"
- "Competitor activity suggests Pro-Tel's focus on [market gap] is well-positioned"
- "Regulatory change creates opportunity for Pro-Tel in [area]"
- "Power infrastructure upgrade opens capacity for Pro-Tel's [target market]"

**Avoid:**
- Promotional language ("Pro-Tel is best positioned...")
- Speculation ("Pro-Tel should consider...")
- Comparisons to competitors (unless factual)

## Power-User Tips

1. **Bulk cite same source:** "This week's activity reflects broader Northeast trends (DCK, EQ, GCP reporting, 03/15-03/22/26)"
2. **Use tables for comparison:** Easier to scan than paragraphs for multiple facilities
3. **Lead with Pro-Tel angle:** Not every story is for DCWU (ask: does Tim care?).
4. **Link strategically:** 3-5 links per email max (more = newsletter fatigue)
5. **Quarterly recaps:** Every 13 weeks, do a "Q1/Q2/Q3/Q4 Recap" — spot trends from 13-week archive

---

## Emergency Edits (If News Breaks Friday Morning)

If a major announcement breaks after 8 AM Friday:
1. Check if Scout has already included it in notes
2. If Scout has: integrate into your draft (cite Scout's prep)
3. If Scout missed: Verify with 2 sources, add to "Breaking This Week" section at top
4. Text Tim immediately if story is time-sensitive (Equinix expansion, major permit, etc.)
